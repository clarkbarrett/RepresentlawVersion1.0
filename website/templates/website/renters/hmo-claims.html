Deposit protection law

All tenants who have paid a deposit to rent flats houses using an assured shorthold tenancy are required to have their deposit protected in a Government Tenancy Deposit scheme.

The 3 tenancy deposit scheme providers your landlord can use. 

  • MyDeposits
  • The Dispute Service
  • The Deposit Protection Service

Tenancy deposits previously held by Capita Tenancy Deposit Service are now protected by mydeposits.

Deposit protection scheme certificate

Your landlord must also send you a certificate confirming that the deposit was registered correctly. This certificate also provide important information that explains what can happen with the deposit and how the deposit protection scheme works.

Your landlord usually has 30 days from when you pay your deposit to: 

  • protect your tenancy deposit with a government-backed scheme
  • tell you which scheme they've used
  • give you certain information about the scheme

If they don't, you can take your landlord to court to claim tenancy deposit compensation.

Compensation can be between 1 and 3 times the amount of your deposit.

Multiple tenancies

Also if you have more than one tenancy with the same landlord and they have failed to protect the deposit when a new tenancy started, you may be entitled to multiple compensation claims.

This can happen if you had a fixed term rental but then stayed beyond the 6 months on a month by month payment basis. In this circumstance, you could claim up to 6 times the deposit paid

You can make a claim for any rental deposit up to 6 years ago as the rule applies to both current and old tenancies. It also does not matter if you were paid your deposit back.
